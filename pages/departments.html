Academic Departments

Our Departments

ELIMS College of Pharmacy has well-established departments staffed by qualified faculty members.

Department of Pharmaceutics

Pharmaceutics is the discipline of pharmacy that deals with turning a new chemical entity (NCE) or existing drug into a safe and effective medication. It is often called the science of dosage form design and links formulation with drug delivery and disposition in the body.

Key Areas

  • Pharmaceutical formulation
  • Pharmaceutical manufacturing
  • Dispensing pharmacy
  • Pharmaceutical technology
  • Physical pharmacy
  • Pharmaceutical jurisprudence

The department focuses on formulation development, novel drug delivery systems, and biopharmaceutics and pharmacokinetics research.

Department of Pharmaceutical Chemistry

Pharmaceutical chemistry studies drug design and synthesis, and the process of bringing new drugs to market. It includes synthesis of medicinal compounds, structure elucidation, and laboratory methods to determine composition qualitatively, semi-qualitatively, and quantitatively.

Key Areas

  • New drug development
  • Computer-aided drug design
  • Structure–activity relationship studies
  • In-vitro and in-vivo screening of chemical moieties

Department of Pharmacognosy

Pharmacognosy is the study of medicinal drugs derived from plants or other natural sources. It examines the physical, chemical, biochemical, and biological properties of natural drugs, and the search for new drugs from natural sources.

Major Fields

  • Ethnopharmacognosy
  • Phytotherapy
  • Phytochemistry

The department builds awareness on medicinal uses of natural drugs, including history, sources, cultivation, active constituents, identification tests, preservation, and adulterants.

Department of Pharmacology

Pharmacology is the branch of biology concerned with drug action and the interaction between living systems and chemicals. It covers pharmacodynamics (drug effects on the body) and pharmacokinetics (ADME).

Key Divisions

  • Clinical pharmacology
  • Neuropharmacology
  • Psychopharmacology
  • Cardiovascular pharmacology
  • Pharmacogenetics and pharmacogenomics
  • Pharmacoepidemiology

Department of Pharmacy Practice

Pharmacy practice is the science of preparing, dispensing, reviewing, and monitoring medications to ensure safe, effective, and affordable use. It includes hospital, community, and clinical pharmacy, and emphasizes evidence-based care, patient safety, and pharmaceutical care.

Key Features

  • Medication therapy management
  • Patient counselling
  • Drug information services
  • Medication reconciliation
  • Pharmacovigilance

For detailed information about each department, please contact the college office.
