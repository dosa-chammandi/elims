Pharm.D — Doctor of Pharmacy

Duration: 6 Years (5 + 1 Internship) Affiliation: KUHS

The Pharm.D (Doctor of Pharmacy) is a postgraduate level integrated course in pharmacy introduced in India by Pharmacy Council of India in 2008 to inculcate a new standard in pharmacy practice. It is a prerequisite to practice as a licensed clinical pharmacist in India and abroad.

Clinical Pharmacists promote appropriate, effective and safe medication use for patients. They go hand in hand with other members of the healthcare team and closely monitor patient drug therapy to make recommendations on selection of best medication for a patient's condition, correct dose and duration of therapy. They also forecast and solve drug-related issues thereby reducing length of hospital stay and economic burden of treatment, thus increasing quality of life of patients.

Pharm.D graduates have excellent earning potential and the profession is consistently ranked as one of the most highly trusted.

Pharm.D is a six-year program after twelfth standard which includes 5 years of academic study and 1 year of internship or residency. Each academic year is spread over a period of not less than 200 working days.

Clinical Training

ELIMS College of Pharmacy, Thrissur has an MOU with Daya General Hospital Ltd, Thrissur and Speciality Surgical Centre, Thrissur where Pharm.D candidates undergo hands-on clinical training under the supervision of clinical preceptors and doctors.

Program Highlights

🏥 Clinical Rotations

Hands-on clinical experience through rotations in partner hospitals and healthcare facilities.

🤝 Hospital MOU

MOU with Daya General Hospital and Speciality Surgical Centre for clinical training.

💊 Patient Care Skills

Training in medication therapy management, patient counselling, and drug safety monitoring.

🔬 Research Training

Exposure to clinical research methodology, drug trials, and evidence-based pharmacy practice.

Eligibility

Candidates who have passed the Higher Secondary Examinations of the Government of Kerala (or examinations recognized as equivalent) with:

  • 50% marks in Biology/Mathematics separately, and
  • 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics put together.

How to Apply

Admission to 50% Merit seats is through the rank list prepared by the Commissioner for Entrance Examinations based on index marks of KEAM. The remaining 50% Management quota is filled by the Kerala State Self Financing Pharmacy College Management Association (KSSPCMA).
